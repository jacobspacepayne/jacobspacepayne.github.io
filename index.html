Jacob Payne

PhD Candidate, Physics and Astronomy
University of Iowa | Iowa City, IA

My research focuses on the intersection of High-Energy Astrophysics and X-ray Instrumentation. I investigate the emission characteristics of pulsars utilizing data from the Neutron star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) mission aboard the International Space Station, aiming to refine our understanding of pulsar timing.

Parallel to my observational work, I contribute to the development of X-ray optics. This involves experimental glass forming to create mirror substrates for multilayer coatings, a method with potential to increasing the reflection efficiency and therefore effective area and sensitivity of future X-ray observatory missions.
